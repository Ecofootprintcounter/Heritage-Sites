Welcome to the Cultural and Natural Heritage Sites of India

About India

India, a land of diverse culture, history, and natural beauty, is home to some of the world's most celebrated heritage and natural sites. These sites are key to preserving India's legacy for generations to come.

Heritage Sites of India

Taj Mahal

click here

Located in Agra, the Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic symbols of India, known for its breathtaking white marble architecture.

Qutub Minar

click here

Situated in Delhi, Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage site known for its towering height and historical significance.

Red Fort

click here

The Red Fort in Delhi is a symbol of India's rich Mughal history and was the residence of the Mughal emperors for around 200 years.

Natural Sites of India

Valley of Flowers

click here

Located in Uttarakhand, the Valley of Flowers is a UNESCO World Heritage site, renowned for its stunning alpine flowers and diverse flora.

Sundarbans

click here

The Sundarbans, located in West Bengal, is a UNESCO World Heritage site famous for its unique mangrove ecosystem and Bengal tigers.

Jim Corbett National Park

click here

Located in Uttarakhand, Jim Corbett National Park is India's first national park and a haven for wildlife, especially tigers.
